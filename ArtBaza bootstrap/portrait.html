Mona Lisa

Painting by Leonardo da Vinci, one of the most famous works of painting. The exact date of writing is unknown (according to some sources, it was written between 1503 and 1505). Now kept in the Louvre. The painting is believed to depict Lisa Gherardini, wife of the Florentine silk merchant Francesco del Giocondo.

Girl with a pearl earring

One of the most famous paintings by the Dutch artist Jan Vermeer. She is often called the Northern or Dutch Mona Lisa. The painting is signed "IVMeer" but not dated. According to the Mauritshuis Museum, where the painting is currently kept, it was painted around 1665. Very little is known about her. It is not known whether Vermeer painted it to order, who in this case was the customer and the name of the girl depicted. According to one version, the artist depicted his own daughter Maria. In any case, it is obvious that this is an unusual portrait.

Adele Bloch-Bauer

Painting by the Austrian artist Gustav Klimt, the most famous of his portraits. Also known as "Golden Adele". The painting is considered one of the most significant works of Austrian Art Nouveau and Gustav Klimt, who in this portrait is at the peak of his "golden period" made a breakthrough into modernity with unprecedented radicalism.

Vertumnus

In 1562, Arcimboldo was invited to the court of the Holy Roman Emperor Maximilian II in Vienna, and then served his successor Rudolf II in Prague. Having served at the Habsburg court for about 25 years, in 1587 the artist retired and returned to Milan. however, he continued to create works in his chosen style, which he sent to Prague.
