Review of the Black Sea fleet

The fleet review took place at the Sevastopol roadstead in 1849. The line of warships of the Black Sea Fleet is headed by the 120-gun battleship "Twelve Apostles", it is followed by the 84-gun Rostislav, Svyatoslav, and Yagudil.

Starnight

One of the most famous paintings by the Dutch post-impressionist artist Vincent van Gogh. Represents a view from the east window of Van Gogh's bedroom in Saint-Rémy-de-Provence of the pre-dawn sky and a fictional village. The painting was painted in June 1889; Since 1941 it has been kept at the Museum of Modern Art in New York. According to the famous astrophysicist Neil Tyson, this is the first picture in which the sky is the main subject of the image.
