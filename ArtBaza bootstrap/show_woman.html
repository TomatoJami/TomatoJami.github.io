Girl with a pearl earring

Year: 1665 Author: Jan Vermeer

Description of Girl with a pearl earring

One of the most famous paintings by the Dutch artist Jan Vermeer. She is often called the Northern or Dutch Mona Lisa. The painting is signed "IVMeer" but not dated. According to the Mauritshuis Museum, where the painting is currently kept, it was painted around 1665. Very little is known about her. It is not known whether Vermeer painted it to order, who in this case was the customer and the name of the girl depicted. According to one version, the artist depicted his own daughter Maria. In any case, it is obvious that this is an unusual portrait. The artist tried to capture the moment when the girl turns her head towards the viewer towards someone she has just noticed.
